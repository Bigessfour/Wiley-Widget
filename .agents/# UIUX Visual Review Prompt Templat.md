# UI/UX Visual Review Prompt Template

**Prompt to copy/paste (replace [PAGE] and [GOAL] as needed):**

Act as a senior UI/UX designer with 12+ years of experience in civic/government websites and conversion optimization for non-technical users. Analyze the attached screenshot of the [PAGE] from the perspective of a real human user (e.g., a Town of Wiley resident looking for quick, trustworthy information on notices, weather, services, or meetings). Do not evaluate as an AI—focus on how it _feels_ to a human scanning the page under time pressure.

**Audience context:** Small-town residents (including elderly users) who value clarity, professionalism, and ease of use. Primary goal is [GOAL, e.g., "quickly finding current notices or signing up for alerts"].

Evaluate and report on:

- **Visual hierarchy & focus:** What are the top 3 elements that draw the eye first? Do they align with the primary user goal? Note any competing elements or visual clutter.
- **Layout, whitespace & scannability:** Identify cramped areas, insufficient padding/margins, or dense sections. Suggest improvements for breathing room, modern feel, and reduced cognitive load.
- **Typography, color palette, imagery & emotional appeal:** Does the design feel welcoming, trustworthy, and professional? Check contrast/readability (especially against backgrounds/images), visual weight, and human psychology. Flag any "bush-league" or unpolished elements.
- **Overall human-centric issues:** How intuitive is the flow? Any areas that increase frustration or confusion for a real person?

Provide 3–5 specific, prioritized, actionable recommendations with clear before/after reasoning. Where helpful, propose simple layout variants (e.g., hero adjustments, card grids, two-column vs single-column) with pros/cons, keeping mobile responsiveness and accessibility (WCAG AA) in mind. Prioritize minimalist, high-contrast, scannable designs that reduce visual noise.

End with a short summary of the overall trustworthiness and appeal score (1–10) and the single highest-impact change.
